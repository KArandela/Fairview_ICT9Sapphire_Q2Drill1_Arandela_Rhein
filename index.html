Emergency Go‑Bag Essentials

Be ready to move in minutes. Pack these core items and review your bag every 6 months.

What is a Go‑Bag?

It’s a pre‑packed bag with supplies for at least 72 hours. Keep it in an easy‑to‑grab place near your exit.

  • ✔ Keep copies of IDs and emergency contacts sealed in a Waterproof pouch.
  • ✔ Rotate food, water, batteries, and meds before they expire.
  • ✔ Customize for kids, pets, seniors, and local hazards.
Hydration

Water

Minimum 3L per person/day. Include purification options.

  • 3× 1L bottles or collapsible bladders
  • Water purification tablets/filter
Calories

Food

No‑cook, long‑life snacks and meals.

  • Energy bars, trail mix
  • Canned goods + opener
Medical

First Aid

Treat minor injuries and stabilize.

  • Bandages, gauze, tape
  • Antiseptic, pain reliever
  • Personal meds (7 days)
Multi‑use Gear

Tools

Compact tools for utility and safety.

  • Flashlight + extra batteries
  • Multi‑tool / duct tape
  • Whistle / lighter / matches

Additional Essentials

Documents

IDs & CASH

  • ID copies, medical info
  • Emergency contacts map
  • Small bills in Waterproof pouch
Clothing

Wear & Warmth

  • Change of clothes, sturdy shoes
  • Rain jacket, thermal blanket
  • Gloves, hat, socks
Hygiene

Sanitation

  • Toothbrush/toothpaste, soap
  • Hand sanitizer, wipes, masks
  • Small towel, tissues
Comms

Tech & Power

  • Power bank + cables
  • Portable radio (NOAA/FM)
  • Backup phone numbers card
Tip: Store your go‑bag near the door. Review contents every 6 months or after use.
